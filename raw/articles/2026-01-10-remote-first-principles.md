Over the last three years at ACME Corp we've moved from office-by-default to remote-by-default. Here's what I've learned.

## 1. Async is the new default

If a decision needs three people to block their calendars, it's probably not ready to be made. We push for decisions to live in writing first — a short doc, a slack thread with a decision log, or (lately) entries in our wiki.

## 2. Writing is the great equalizer

The best-written idea wins, not the loudest voice in the room. We've stopped doing status meetings almost entirely. The weekly digest is a doc, read asynchronously, with comments.

## 3. Meetings are for alignment, not information transfer

When we do meet, we meet to align on trade-offs or get unblocked on something genuinely ambiguous. Status updates and information transfer happen in writing.

## 4. Build for the worst time zone

If your team spans 8 time zones, design for the person most disadvantaged, not the majority. All critical artifacts must be accessible async.

## 5. Invest in tooling relentlessly

We spent the equivalent of two engineering months in 2025 just on our internal tooling for writing, search, and decision-tracking. Paid back in three months.

---

## Takeaways
- Default to written, async, decision-driven communication.
- Meetings are a tool for ambiguity resolution, not broadcast.
- Invest in writing/search tooling — it compounds.
